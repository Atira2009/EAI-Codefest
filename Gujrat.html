Leatherback Sea Turtle

The leatherback sea turtle, sometimes called the lute turtle or leathery turtle ,is scientificaly called Dermochelys coriacea. This is endangered because of plastic which may look like a jelly fish so they eat it , and because of the poaching of their eggs.

Leatherback Sea Turtle are known as gelatinivores , which means they only eat invertebrates such as jellyfish and sea squirts.

Conservation status - Vulnerable


Greater Adjutant-Stork

The Greater Adjutant-Stork is a part of the stork family,it is scientificaly called the Leptoptilos dubius.it is endangered due to loss of nesting and feeding habbits through pollution and other disturbances .

The Greater Adjutant-Stork is an omnivorous , it is mainly a scavenger. it preys on frogs and large insects, birds, reptiles and rodents.

Conservation status - Endangered


Sociable Lapwing

The Sociable Lapwing is an endangered wader in the lapwing family of birds. It is scientificaly called Vanellus gregarius .It is endangered due to hunting pressure on the migration route .

The Socible Lapwing feeds on insects such as grasshoppers, beetles, crickets and moth larvae.

Conservation status- Critically Endangered
