Endangered Animals of Karnataka

Endangered Animals of

Karnataka

Sambar Deer

The sambar (Rusa unicolor) is a large deer that is native to the Indian subcontinent, South China and Southeast Asia. Its conservation status is vulnerable. A reason why the sambar deer's numbers are declining is hunting.

Four-horned Antelope

The four-horned antelope (Tetracerus quadricornis) is also known as the chousingha. It is found in India and Nepal. Its conservation status is vulnerable. It is endangered due to the loss of its natural habitat and hunting.

Rusty-spotted Cat

The rusty-spotted cat (Prionailurus rubiginosus) is found in India, Sri Lanka and Nepal. Its conservation status is near threatened. The global wild population of the Rusty-spotted cat is affected by the loss and destruction of deciduous forests, its prime habitat
