Endangered Animals of Haryana

Endangered Animals of

Haryana

Indian Softshell Turtle

The Indian softshell turtle (Nilssonia gangetica), also known as the Ganges softshell turtle is found in South Asian rivers such as the Ganges, Indus and the Mahanadi. Its conservation status is vulnerable. It eats fish, amphibians, other animal matter and aquatic plants. Some reasons why it is threatened are the introduction of dams and overfishing.

Indian Vulture

The Indian vulture (Gyps indicus) is native to India, Pakistan and Nepal. It breeds on hilly crags. Its conservation status is critically endangered. It is a scavenger. Its numbers declined because of diclofenac (a vetrinary drug) poisoning.

White Rumped Vulture

The White-rumped Vulture (Gyps bengalensis) is native to South and Southeast Asia. They nest in large trees. Its conservation status is critically endangered. It is also a scavenger. The reason for the decline of its population is also due to diclofenac (a vetrinary drug) poisoning.

Conservation Efforts

Species like the blackbuck, chinkara, vultures and indian softshelled turtles are being protected through conservation and breeding efforts in Haryana.
