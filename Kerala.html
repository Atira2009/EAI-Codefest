Endangered Animals of Kerala

Endangered Animals of

Kerala

Nilgiri Tahr

The Nilgiri tahr (Nilgirtragus hylocrius) lives in the mountains in Southern India. There are about 1800 to 2000 of them left. It is an herbivore. Their conservation status is endangered. Its numbers decreased due to hunting and poaching.

Grizzled Giant Squirrel

The grizzled giant squirrel (Ratufa macroura) is a large tree squirrel found in Sri Lanka, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and Kerala. It is an omnivore. Their conservation status is near threatened. They are listed as near threatened because of habitat loss and hunting.

Leopard

The leopard (Panthera pardus) is found in Africa, Asia and Europe. They are nocturnal They are carnivores. Their conservation status is vulnerable. Some threats to them are habitat fragmentation and poaching.

Conservation Efforts

There are five National Parks in Kerala for the conservation of biodiversity and fifteen wildlife sanctuaries.
